Robert Nester A.K.A. "Bob" Marley

An Icon of Peace and Unity

Mr. Marley pictured here uniting two apposing polotical parties in a turbulant election year for Jamaica.

Some important dates to remember some of Bob's achievments.

  • 1945- Bob Marley was born 6 February 1945 on the farm of his maternal grandfather in Nine Mile, Saint Ann Parish, Jamaica.
  • 1962- The first four songs Bob records: "Judge Not", "One Cup of Coffee", "Do You Still Love Me?" and "Terror", at Federal Studio for local music producer Leslie Kong.
  • 1972- Bob signs with CBS Records located in the United Kingdom.
  • 1978- In 1978, Marley returned to Jamaica and performed at another political concert, the One Love Peace Concert, again in an effort to calm warring parties.
  • 1979- "Survival, a defiant and politically charged album, was released in 1979. Tracks such as "Zimbabwe", "Africa Unite", "Wake Up and Live", and "Survival" reflected Marley's support for the struggles of Africans. His appearance at the Amandla Festival in Boston in July 1979 showed his strong opposition to South African apartheid..."
